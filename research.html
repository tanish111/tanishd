Research

GPU Power Cap Optimization (HPDC '25 – Best Poster Award) ACM HPDC '25 Symposium, Notre Dame, USA
2025
  • Designed an ML-driven framework for dynamic GPU power cap tuning, targeting performance-per-watt maximization under strict energy budgets.
  • Evaluated on 20+ workloads from Rodinia and Parboil, the system delivered up to 18% higher throughput compared to NVIDIA's default power management, while maintaining the same average power draw.
  • In multi-GPU cluster tests, this translated to 15–20% energy savings per node, which at HPC data center scale can mean millions of dollars in yearly savings.
  • This work earned the Best Poster Award at HPDC '25 and highlighted the role of AI in sustainable high-performance computing.
GPU Power Consumption Prediction (ICTAI '24) 36th IEEE ICTAI
2024
  • Built a static analysis–based prediction framework for GPU power usage that avoids the need for physical measurements.
  • Tested tree-based models, neural networks, and domain adaptation methods across six CUDA benchmark suites.
  • The best-performing approach, domain adaptation, achieved >90% prediction accuracy when generalizing from older GPUs (Pascal, Volta) to newer NVIDIA architectures (Ampere, Hopper).
  • This reduces the profiling time from hours of hardware measurements to a few seconds of static analysis, directly benefiting both researchers optimizing kernels and data centers monitoring energy efficiency.
GPU Temperature Prediction (HiPC '24) 31st IEEE HiPC
2024
  • Developed an LSTM-based model to forecast GPU temperature by capturing complex temporal patterns in workload behavior.
  • Unlike static models, this approach adapts to runtime variations, providing more accurate and fine-grained predictions.
  • The impact is in proactive thermal management—reducing throttling, improving reliability, and extending hardware lifespan for data centers and HPC clusters.
